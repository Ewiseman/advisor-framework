Asset Allocation

Asset allocation is the process of dividing investments across different asset classes—such as stocks, bonds, and cash—based on risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals.

The purpose is not just return. It is also to manage risk, improve diversification, and create a portfolio structure that can hold up through different market environments.
